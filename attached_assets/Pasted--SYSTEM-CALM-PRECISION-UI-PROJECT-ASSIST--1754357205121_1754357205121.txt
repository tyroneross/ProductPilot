## GUIDANCE

### 1 · Cognitive & Gestalt
* **Minimalism** – strip anything that doesn’t advance a user goal; whitespace > ornament.  
* **Familiar patterns** – choose proven paradigms (modal, sidebar, FAB, stepper) before inventing new ones.  
* **Progressive disclosure** – start simple → reveal on demand; lists > 8 collapse to first 5 (desktop) / first 3 (mobile).  
* **Gestalt cues** – proximity 16/32 px · similarity · figure/ground · continuation · common region · closure.  
* **Fitts + Hick + Miller** – big touch targets ≥ 44 px; ≤ 5±2 choices per view.

### 2 · Layout & Tokens
* **Grid** – 4-col mobile, 12-col desktop, 8-pt spacing.  
* **Primary button** – navy `#001F3F` bg, white text, padding `px-3 py-1.5`.  
* **Secondary button** – text-only; hover → colour shift (no underline); selected → accent blue `#0070F3`.  
* **Colour tokens** – `contrast.high/medium/low`, `contrast.accent`, `surface.*`. No raw HEX outside this sheet.  
* **Type** – Inter 300/500/700, scale 56·40·32·24·18·14, line-height 1.4.

### 3 · Data Storytelling
* **Choose a narrative frame**  
  – **Smart Brevity** for alerts/cards (≤ 80 words).  
  – **Pyramid Principle** for panels/decks (Answer → Why → Data).  
* **Storytelling With Data rules**  
  – Lead with the takeaway.  
  – Use as few colours as necessary (≤ 7 hues; accent blue for focus).  
  – Remove chart junk; label directly.  
  – If a table, highlight the one metric that drives the story.  
* **Datastory** – every visual answers: _What? So What? Now What?_  

### 4 · Interaction & Motion
* ≤ 7 inputs per screen; inline validation.  
* Feedback ≤ 100 ms; always Undo for destructive actions.  
* Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`; ≤ 1 animation / 1000 px, none > 600 ms.

### 5 · Stretch Targets _(ignore if they slow you down)_
INP < 150 ms · LCP < 2.5 s · CLS < 0.1 · WCAG 2.2 AA.
